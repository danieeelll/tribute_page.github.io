Ján Kuciak (17 May 1990 – 21 February 2018) was a Slovak investigative journalist. He worked as a reporter for the news website Aktuality.sk, focused mainly on investigating tax fraud of several businessmen with connections to top-level Slovak politicians. He and his fiancée Martina Kušnírová were shot dead in February 2018 in their home.

Jan Kuciak was the first journalist murdered in Slovakia since the country's independence. The murders caused shock and disbelief throughout the country, sparking mass popular protests and a political crisis. The disgusting mafia murder of two innocent young people changed the whole nation forever.

The biggests people's protests in the country's history took place. Slovaks living abroad organise gatherings to support their country. Protesters demand a fair investigation of the murder, the resignation of Police Corps President, Interior Minister and Prime Minister. The crisis culminated on 15 March with the resignation of Prime Minister Robert Fico and his entire cabinet. The corrupted and crime-covered government has fallen.

Political parties with new priorities based on "transparency, zero tolerance for corruption or familyhood" won the elections. But the major change has taken place in the minds of people who are no longer willing to tolerate corruption and crime in general.
